Chapter 1: The Rise of Nationalism in Europe

Short Notes

Nationalism: A feeling of unity and pride among people of a nation.

French Revolution (1789): Introduced ideas of liberty, equality and fraternity. Sovereignty shifted from king to people.

Napoleonic Code (1804): Established equality before law and ended feudal system.

Vienna Congress (1815): Organized by Metternich to restore monarchies in Europe.

Unification of Italy: Led by Mazzini, Cavour and Garibaldi.

Unification of Germany: Achieved in 1871 under Otto von Bismarck.

Balkan Region: Became a centre of nationalist tensions leading to World War I.

Short Answer Questions

Q1. What is a nation?
A nation is a group of people who share common culture, history and territory.

Q2. What were the effects of the French Revolution?
It promoted equality, liberty and nationalism across Europe.

Q3. Who were the leaders of Italian unification?
Mazzini, Cavour and Garibaldi.

Q4. What was the aim of the Vienna Congress?
To restore monarchies and maintain peace in Europe.

Q5. What was Bismarck’s policy?
He followed the policy of “Blood and Iron” to unify Germany.

Long Answer Questions

Q1. Explain the unification of Germany.
Germany was divided into many small states. Otto von Bismarck led three wars against Denmark, Austria and France. In 1871, Germany was unified and Kaiser William I was declared Emperor.

Q2. Describe the role of the French Revolution in nationalism.
The French Revolution ended monarchy rule and introduced equality, liberty and fraternity. It inspired nationalist movements across Europe.

Q3. Explain the Balkan problem.
The Balkan region had different ethnic groups. Nationalism created conflicts among them, which later became a major cause of World War I.
