Chapter 2 – Nationalism in India

Short Notes

1. First World War (1914–18): Taxes increased, prices rose, forced recruitment happened. People became unhappy with British rule.

2. Rowlatt Act (1919): Allowed arrest without trial. Gandhi started protest against it.

3. Jallianwala Bagh (1919): General Dyer fired on peaceful crowd in Amritsar. Hundreds died.

4. Non-Cooperation Movement (1920): Boycott of schools, courts, foreign goods.

5. Khilafat Movement: Led by Ali Brothers to protect Khalifa of Turkey.

6. Chauri Chaura Incident (1922): Protest became violent; Gandhi withdrew movement.

7. Civil Disobedience Movement (1930): Gandhi broke salt law through Dandi March.

8. Simon Commission (1928): No Indian member. Protest slogan: “Simon Go Back”.

9. Poona Pact (1932): Agreement between Gandhi and Dr. B.R. Ambedkar.

10. Role of Women: Women actively participated in national movements.

10 Short Answer Questions

1. What was Rowlatt Act?
It allowed arrest without trial.

2. What happened at Jallianwala Bagh?
General Dyer ordered firing on peaceful crowd.

3. Why was Non-Cooperation Movement started?
To protest against British injustice.

4. What was Dandi March?
Gandhi marched to break salt law.

5. Who were Ali Brothers?
Leaders of Khilafat Movement.

6. What was Simon Commission?
A British commission with no Indian member.

7. What is Civil Disobedience?
Peaceful breaking of unjust laws.

8. What was Poona Pact?
Agreement on representation of depressed classes.

9. What is Swaraj?
Self-rule.

10. Why was Chauri Chaura important?
It led Gandhi to withdraw Non-Cooperation.

5 Long Answer Questions

1. Explain Non-Cooperation Movement.
Launched in 1920 by Gandhi. People boycotted British institutions and goods.

2. Describe Civil Disobedience Movement.
Started in 1930 with Dandi March. People broke salt law and protested peacefully.

3. What were effects of First World War on India?
Heavy taxes, price rise, forced recruitment created anger among Indians.

4. Explain role of women in national movement.
Women participated in protests, marches and boycotts.

5. What was significance of Jallianwala Bagh?
It united Indians against British rule.
